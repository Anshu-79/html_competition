INTRODUCTION

Pinewood School Saharanpur is a minority educational institution, founded by Late
Mrs. Pritilata Talibuddin in January 1966 in response to the need of that time for an
English Medium Primary School. Now the School has attained the status of 10+2 and is
affiliated to CBSE New Delhi and has more than 2700 students on its rolls and about 125
highly qualified and dedicated teaching staff. It is a well reputed English Medium
Co-Educational School with a very impressive building. It has airy and spacious class rooms
and is located in a pollution free and healthy environment. It has had 100% Board results
with all First Divisions. There is a common course for all students up to Class X and
thereafter the courses are split into Science, Commerce and Humanities Streams.
The Pinewood Emblem is an adaptation of the Star of India. It comprises of thirty two rays
representing the points of the compass which symbolize the universality of the institution.
In the centre there is an oval field, divided into three sectors by the Ganga and Yamuna 'do-abb'
and in each sector there is a pine tree representing the Prime Virtues of Faith, Hope and
Charity. The Central field is enclosed within a 'Belt of Protection' emblazoned with the
motto "Heaven's Light Our Guide".
